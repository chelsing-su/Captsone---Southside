Projects Overview

The Southside Community Coalition is working on several projects in the Syracuse Community. These projects serve the purpose of restoring, revitalizing, and rejuvenating the Syracuse's south side community. They all have sustainable and long term benefits for the community. These projects are being worked on by community residents who have taken initiative to make sure that their community has a brighter future. Please take a look at all the projects that we are currently involved in. We are always looking for help so feel free to get involved and contact us.

Take a look at some of the great work we've been able to accomplish alongside other great organizations in our community!

Food Cooperative

This project is in response to the poor quality and high price of foods currently available on the South Side of Syracuse. The Food Cooperative is dedicated to bringing high-quality, reasonably priced foods/products to the residents of the Syracuse’s South Side. This project was made possible through a partnership and collaborative effort between the SCC, SU Southside Initiative and SU College of Law’s Community Development Law Clinic, Ground Works Capital Coalition (GC2), and Syracuse University’s Whitman School of Management Entrepreneurship and Emerging Enterprise program, Allyn foundation, CNY Community Foundation, Gifford Foundation, New York State Empire Development and with the support of the City Of Syracuse. During the initial developmental stages the partnership encouraged community interest in the project by sponsoring Store- for -a- Day and Living Room events/presentations. The initial development of the Food Cooperative is complete and once the structure is completed the food coop "Eat to Live" will move into its home at 2327 S. Salina Street in 2013.

FilmFest

The South Side Film Festival brings people of all ages together for a free outdoor movie for four evenings every summer. Co-sponsored by the SCC, SU South Side Initiative, Visual Technologies and Key Bank, the annual event features films that are family oriented. Films are shown in the parking lot of Key Bank, located at the corner of South Salina and East Colvin streets. The festival was launched in 2007 to enhance cultural opportunities on Syracuse’s South Side, promote neighborhood goods and services, and foster family and community engagement around a positive event. Among the films shown in past years: Akeelah and the Bee, The Great Debaters, and The Express. Refreshments—including popcorn, soda, and ice cream are available for purchase from local vendors. The event eventually will evolve into more of a traditional film festival, dedicated to showcasing original works by local, national, and international filmmakers.

Music and Arts Festival

A joint venture between the SCC and Syracuse University’s South Side Initiative, this event was created for the purpose of providing a venue for entertainers, artists and local businesses to interact while showcasing the positive energy of the South Side community. The free, public outdoor event took place in 2006 and 2007 at the corner of South Salina and Colvin streets. Sponsors of the events included Clear Channel Radio, Onondaga County, the City of Syracuse, Central New York Community Foundation, Syracuse University, National Grid and the SCC.

Salina Electronics Village

This project is the manifestation of the dialogue between the faculty of the SU School of Information Studies and community leaders about the lack of computer literacy and digital access on the South Side of Syracuse. The electronic village projects consists of a technology initiative, developed by Professor Murali Venkatesh of the School of Information Studies at Syracuse University, in order to inform South Side residents about computer technologies, for the purpose of creating employment and boosting communication opportunities. The electronic village projects include: the Side Communications Center, Stand newspaper, the Wireless project, Community Technology Workshops, Networking Academy classes and Digital Access Day.

South Side Communication Center

The South Side Communication Center serves as an open access computer cluster providing residents with free public access to computers with internet connectivity and on-site assistance. It will be a central base for community unification while increasing the community’s computer resources. The center provides a platform for several SCC and Syracuse University’s South Side Initiative projects, including The Stand newspaper and the Networking Academy classes, respectively. Syracuse community businesses and organizations are able to reserve meeting and workshop space in the South Side Communication Center.

"The Stand" Newspaper

The Stand newspaper project provides clear and balanced reporting of news and information for the South Side community of Syracuse. Contents include local and international news, community calendar/coverage of events, and information related to community empowerment. What makes The Stand newspaper unique is that it is a community newspaper that seeks to give a voice to the voiceless. In the near future The Stand will be self-sustaining, owned and operated by residents of the South Side community. The Stand newspaper currently offers an online as well as print edition with reasonably priced advertisement options. Collaboration involves SCC, SU Southside Initiative Office and SU Newhouse School of Public Communications.
